How to read this. Distances (common tones, voice-leading) are computed in pure pitch-class space — twelve objects, enharmonics collapsed — which keeps the core universal and temperament-grounded. Function (does this resolve like a dominant, or expand like an augmented sixth?) lives in the spelled layer beneath each result. Each connector now builds a full path: the predominant slot tries both ii and IV and keeps whichever voice-leads more smoothly, and the cadence stays mode-appropriate (no foreign leading tone forced into a modal target). The inversion line under each chord is the bass note that minimises total bass motion across the chain — solved as a shortest-path over the progression — with the source, the cadence, and the final tonic pinned to root position for cadential weight and 6/4s penalised per convention. Infer source from melody deduces the most likely starting key/mode from the notes alone by weighting each note's scale-degree stability against every candidate collection. "Exhaustive" still means exhaustive within a triad-plus-seventh vocabulary; widening it grows the candidate set but not the method.
